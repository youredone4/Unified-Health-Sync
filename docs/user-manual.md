# HealthSync — User Manual

A friendly, click-by-click guide for everyone who uses HealthSync — barangay
nurses (Team Leaders), Municipal Health Officers, Senior Health Administrators,
the Mayor, and the Health Committee. No technical knowledge needed.

If you can read and click, you can use this guide.

## 1 · Welcome

HealthSync is the digital record book and dashboard for your barangay's and
municipality's health programs. Think of it as a giant, organized notebook
shared by every Team Leader and the RHU office. It replaces the stacks of
yellow registers and the manual M1 form at the end of each month — once you
record patients in HealthSync as part of your daily work, the M1 report
fills itself in.

**The promise:** any data you capture once (a new mother, a vaccine, a TB
dose) shows up everywhere else it's needed — in the worklist, the dashboards,
the monthly report, and the alerts the Municipal Health Officer sees.

## 2 · Logging in for the first time

1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge — any of them).
2. Type the address your IT person gave you in the URL bar at the top, and press **Enter**.
3. You will see the **Sign in** page with a HealthSync logo.
4. Type your **username** in the first box.
5. Type your **password** in the second box.
6. Click the blue **Sign in** button.
7. If your username and password are correct, the home page opens. If not, an error message appears in red — try again, or ask your administrator to reset your password.

> **Tip:** If you forget your password, do not try to guess. Ask your administrator (the SYSTEM_ADMIN, often the IT focal at the RHU) to reset it.

## 3 · Your role and what you can do

Every user has a **role**. The role decides which menu items you see and what you can change. There are six roles. Yours is shown on the **Account** page (top-right corner of the screen, click your name).

| Your role | What it means | What you mostly do |
|---|---|---|
| **Team Leader (TL)** | Barangay nurse — captures the day's records | Add mothers, children, seniors, TB patients, vaccines, screenings; submit weekly PIDSR; submit your M1 |
| **Municipal Health Officer (MHO)** | RHU physician | See ALL barangays; review escalated cases; approve M1; manage outbreak responses |
| **Senior Health Administrator (SHA)** | Second in command to the MHO — the RHU's administrative officer | Same access as MHO for most pages; oversees operations, approvals, and day-to-day administration across barangays |
| **System Administrator** | The IT focal | Create user accounts, change settings, see audit logs, access everything |
| **Mayor** | Read-only municipal view | Look at dashboards and reports; cannot change records |
| **Health Committee** | Read-only oversight view | Same as Mayor |

**Important:** if a menu item is missing from your sidebar, that means your role is not allowed to use it. That is normal — it's not a bug.

> **For Team Leaders:** you only see records from the barangay (or barangays) assigned to you. You will not see your neighbor barangay's patients. The Municipal Health Officer sees all barangays.

## 4 · The home screen, explained

After login, the page that appears depends on your role:

### If you are a Team Leader

You see **Today** — your personal worklist for the day.

- **Top of the screen:** a card called "Recent DOH updates" with the latest memos and advisories from the regional DOH office. Click any title to read more.
- **Below that:** an "Action queue" — colored badges showing how many patients need attention right now. For example: *3 PNC visits overdue*, *5 TB doses today*, *2 vaccines due*.
- **Click any badge** → it takes you to the matching worklist with those patients pre-filtered.

### If you are MHO, SHA, or Administrator

You see the **Municipal Overview dashboard** — a wide page with totals across all barangays.

- **Top:** the same "Recent DOH updates" card.
- **Below:** key numbers (total active mothers, children due for vaccines, TB patients with missed doses, barangays with stockouts, etc.).
- Each tile is **clickable** and drills into the matching list.

## 5 · Finding your way around

The **left sidebar** is the main menu. Click the three-line icon at the top to expand or collapse it.

The sidebar is organized into groups. Click a group header to open or close it.

| Sidebar group | What's inside |
|---|---|
| **Today** (top) | Your daily action queue |
| **Patients** | Mothers, Children, Seniors, TB DOTS — the four core registers |
| **Daily Operations** | The clinical pages a Team Leader touches every day (ANC, PNC, vaccines, walk-ins, etc.) |
| **Registries & Surveillance** | Periodic registers — Disease Surveillance, PIDSR, Outbreaks, Mortality, AEFI, Cold Chain |
| **Services** | Patient-facing services — Konsulta, Referrals, Certificates, Campaigns |
| **Pharmacy** | Inventory, Stockouts, Dispensings, Restock Requests |
| **Dashboards** | Visual summaries (Maternal, Child, Senior, Nutrition, Disease Map, Hotspots) |
| **Reports** | Generate the M1 and other monthly reports |
| **Calendar** | Scheduled events (vaccines, ANC visits, TB doses) |
| **DOH Updates** | All memos and advisories |
| **Glossary** | Definitions of every medical acronym used in the app |
| **MGMT Inbox** | (officers only) Centralized review queue |
| **Workforce** | (officers only) Health workers, licenses |
| **Messages** | SMS outbox and threads |
| **Admin** | (administrator only) Users, Audit Logs, Recommendation Calibration, Settings |

**Top right corner** holds your name, notifications, and the barangay selector (if you cover more than one barangay).

> **Tip:** every page has a title at the top with breadcrumbs (small text like *Patients › Mothers › Maria Santos*). Click any breadcrumb to go back.

## 6 · Mothers

### What this is for

Tracking pregnant and postpartum women: their ANC visits, screenings, deliveries, and PNC visits — everything the M1 form's Sections A, B, and C asks for.

### Where to find it

Sidebar → **Patients** group → click **Mothers**. This opens the **Mothers Hub** with five tabs across the top: **Prenatal · Prenatal Screenings · PNC · Birth Attendance · FP**.

### Adding a new pregnant mother (most common task)

1. From the **Prenatal** tab click the blue **+ Add Mother** button at the top right.
2. A new page opens called "Register New Mother".
3. Fill in:
   - **First Name** and **Last Name** (required)
   - **Age** (required)
   - **Barangay** — usually pre-selected to yours
   - **LMP date** (Last Menstrual Period) — a calendar opens when you click the box
   - **EDC date** (Expected Date of Confinement) — usually fills in automatically from LMP
   - **ANC visits so far** (how many prenatal check-ups she has had with you)
   - **TT doses** (tetanus toxoid 1, 2, 3, 4, or 5)
   - **Phone number** (for SMS reminders)
4. Click the green **Save** button at the bottom.
5. A green popup at the top says "Mother registered successfully" and the page goes back to the worklist. Her name now appears at the top of the **Active mothers** list.

### Logging an ANC visit

1. From the Prenatal worklist click on the mother's name.
2. Her **profile page** opens.
3. Find the card called **Visit history** or **ANC visits**.
4. Click **+ New Visit**.
5. Fill in:
   - **Visit date**
   - **Blood pressure** (systolic/diastolic)
   - **Weight** in kg
   - **Fetal heart tone** (yes/no, beats per minute if measured)
   - **Notes** (anything important)
6. Click **Save**. The visit appears in the list with the date.

> **What to expect:** as ANC visits accumulate, the M1 indicator A-01 (mothers with ≥4 ANC visits) updates automatically — you do not edit M1 by hand.

### Recording prenatal screenings (Hep B, Anemia, GDM, etc.)

1. Mothers Hub → **Prenatal Screenings** tab.
2. Find the mother's row. If she's not yet in the list, register her first (see above).
3. Click her row. A side panel slides in.
4. Tick the boxes for screenings she has completed: Hep B, Anemia, Iron/Folic, MMS, Calcium, Deworming, GDM screening.
5. Click **Save**.

### Logging a delivery (Birth Attendance)

1. Mothers Hub → **Birth Attendance** tab.
2. Click **+ New Birth**.
3. Fill in:
   - Mother (search by name)
   - Delivery date
   - **Attended by** (Physician / Nurse / Midwife / Other)
   - **Delivery type** (Vaginal / Cesarean — and Full term / Pre-term, or Fetal death / Abortion)
4. **Save**. The mother now appears in the **PNC** tab.

### Logging a PNC visit

1. Mothers Hub → **PNC** tab.
2. The list shows postpartum mothers and which PNC visits they've had (24 hours, 72 hours, 7 days, 6 weeks).
3. Click a mother's row. A side panel opens.
4. Tick the visit you just performed (e.g., **2nd PNC complete**).
5. If she came from another barangay or LGU, tick **TRANS-IN**. If she moved away, tick **TRANS-OUT**.
6. **Save**.

### Family Planning records

1. Mothers Hub → **FP** tab.
2. **+ Add FP Record** at the top right.
3. Fill in:
   - Patient (search; if she's a registered mother, use her record)
   - **Method** (Pills / IUD / Implant / Condom / Sterilization / Injectable)
   - **Start date**
   - **Side effects** (text box, optional)
4. **Save**. Modern methods are counted in the M1 FP-00 indicator.

## 7 · Children

### What this is for

The under-5 register: vaccines, growth monitoring, sick-child visits, vitamin A doses.

### Where to find it

Sidebar → **Patients** group → **Children**. Tabs at the top: **Children Worklist · Sick Child**.

### Registering a new child

1. From the worklist click **+ Add Child** at the top right.
2. Fill in:
   - **First name** and **Last name**
   - **Date of birth** (calendar opens)
   - **Sex** (Male / Female)
   - **Mother** (search and link if she's already registered as a mother — optional)
   - **Barangay**
   - **Birth weight** in kilograms (a child under 2.5 kg is "Low Birth Weight" and triggers iron supplementation in M1)
3. **Save**. The child appears at the top of the worklist.

### Recording a vaccine dose

1. From the worklist click on the child's name → child profile opens.
2. Find the **Vaccination card** at the top.
3. Each empty box is a vaccine slot (BCG, Penta 1/2/3, OPV, IPV, MR, etc.).
4. Click an empty box.
5. Fill in **Date given** and **Lot number** (the vial label).
6. **Save**. The box turns green and the child's vaccination status updates.

> **Tip:** if the lot number is rejected because it's not in the cold-chain log, ask the person who recorded the cold-chain readings that day to log the new lot first.

### Recording growth (weight, height, MUAC)

1. From the worklist click on the child's name.
2. Find the **Growth log** card.
3. Click **+ New Reading**.
4. Fill in **Date**, **Weight (kg)**, **Height (cm)**, **MUAC (cm)** if measured.
5. **Save**. The app calculates Weight-for-Height z-score automatically and flags **MAM** (Moderate Acute Malnutrition) or **SAM** (Severe Acute Malnutrition) if applicable.

### Logging a sick-child visit

1. Sidebar → **Daily Operations** → **Sick Child** (or Children Hub → Sick Child tab).
2. Click **+ New Sick Child Visit**.
3. Fill in:
   - Child (search)
   - **Visit date**
   - **Danger signs** (tick all that apply: not able to drink, vomits everything, convulsions, lethargic)
   - **Main symptoms** (cough, diarrhea, fever, ear problem, etc.)
   - **Classification** (Pneumonia / Diarrhea / Malaria / etc.) — based on IMCI
   - **Treatment given**
4. **Save**.

### Nutrition (MAM / SAM follow-ups)

1. Sidebar → **Daily Operations** → **Nutrition**.
2. Tabs: **Worklist** (active malnutrition cases) · **Growth Monitoring** (z-score charts).
3. Click any child row to record:
   - **SFP enrollment** (Supplementary Feeding Program)
   - **OTC enrollment** (Outpatient Therapeutic Care, for SAM)
   - **Follow-up outcome** (Cured / Non-cured / Defaulted)

## 8 · Seniors

### What this is for

Tracking patients 60 and over: blood pressure, medications, vision, PhilPEN risk assessment.

### Where to find it

Sidebar → **Patients** → **Seniors**.

### Registering a new senior

1. Worklist → **+ Add Senior**.
2. Fill in name, date of birth, sex, barangay, blood pressure (first reading), medications they're already taking, comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension).
3. **Save**.

### Recording a blood pressure reading

1. Click the senior's name → profile opens.
2. **BP Log** card → **+ New Reading**.
3. Fill in date, systolic, diastolic.
4. **Save**. If BP is high, the senior turns red on the worklist.

### Linking to vision and PhilPEN

The senior profile shows quick-link buttons to:
- **Vision Screening** → opens a pre-filled vision form for that senior.
- **PhilPEN Assessment** → opens a pre-filled risk-factor form.

## 9 · TB DOTS

### What this is for

Tracking tuberculosis patients on Directly Observed Treatment, Short-course. Every dose, every visit.

### Where to find it

Sidebar → **Patients** → **TB DOTS**.

### Registering a new TB patient

1. Worklist → **+ New TB Patient**.
2. Fill in:
   - Demographics (name, age, barangay, address, phone)
   - **TB type** (Pulmonary / Extra-pulmonary)
   - **Treatment phase** (Intensive / Continuation)
   - **Treatment start date**
   - **Medication regimen** (e.g. 2HRZE/4HR)
3. **Save**.

### Recording a daily DOTS dose

1. Worklist → click the patient's name.
2. **DOTS Schedule** card shows the next visit date and treatment progress.
3. **Dose log** card → tick today's dose as **Given**, or mark **Missed** with a reason.
4. The system updates "missed doses count" automatically. After 3 missed doses, the patient turns red on the worklist (urgent).

### Sending a DOTS visit reminder by SMS

1. From the patient's profile click the **Tools** menu (three dots, top right).
2. Choose **Send DOTS reminder**.
3. A pre-written message appears with the patient's next visit date.
4. Edit if needed, then click **Send**. The SMS goes out and a record appears in **Messages**.

## 10 · Disease Surveillance

### What this is for

Five vertical disease programs that need their own register: Filariasis, Rabies, Schistosomiasis, Soil-Transmitted Helminth (STH), and Leprosy.

### Where to find it

Sidebar → **Registries & Surveillance** → **Disease Surveillance**. Tabs across the top: **Filariasis · Rabies · Schisto · STH · Leprosy**.

### Recording a Rabies exposure (most common)

1. Click the **Rabies** tab.
2. Fill in:
   - Patient (name, date of birth, sex)
   - **Exposure date**
   - **Category**:
     - **I** — touching/feeding animal, intact skin → no risk
     - **II** — minor scratches/abrasions, no bleeding → vaccine only
     - **III** — bites that broke skin, mucosa exposure, bat contact → MEDICAL EMERGENCY (vaccine + RIG + ABTC referral)
   - **Treatment center** (ABTC / Non-ABTC)
   - **Complete doses** ✓ if all five anti-rabies vaccine doses were given
3. **Save**. The row appears in the table below.

### Reviewing a row (this is the powerful part)

1. Click any row in any of the five tabs.
2. A side panel opens called the **Action drawer**.
3. **At the top, you might see a coloured recommendation card.** This is the system suggesting what to do, based on DOH protocols. Examples:
   - **Red urgent card** for Cat III rabies — vaccine schedule + RIG + ABTC referral
   - **Amber advisory card** for confirmed STH — schedule school deworming
   - **Amber advisory card** for new leprosy case — start contact tracing + MDT
4. Each card lists the bullets you should do, the DOH source it came from, and a footer that says *"DOH guidance — not a clinical order. Reviewer judgment required."*
5. **Click "Show plain language"** at the bottom of the card → a 2-4 sentence explanation appears in plain Filipino-English. Useful for explaining to family members or the Mayor.
6. **Status dropdown** below the card: change from **REPORTED** to **REVIEWED** (you saw it), **ESCALATED** (needs MHO attention), or **CLOSED** (case complete).
7. **Reviewer notes** — what you decided / what you did.
8. Click **Save**. The drawer closes and the row's status badge updates.

> **What does the red banner above the bullets mean?** If 3 or more Category III rabies exposures happened in the same barangay within 7 days, a red **cluster alert** banner appears: *"Possible cluster — consider raising an outbreak alert."*

## 11 · PIDSR

### What this is for

The weekly notifiable-disease attestation under RA 11332. Every Friday, every barangay must submit "zero cases this week" or list cases for diseases like AFP, Measles, Neonatal Tetanus, Human Rabies, Cholera, Anthrax, etc.

### Where to find it

Sidebar → **Registries & Surveillance** → **PIDSR**.

### Submitting this week (Team Leaders, every Friday)

1. The page shows a button at the top right: **+ Submit this week**.
2. Click it. A dialog opens listing the diseases.
3. For each disease, tick **Zero report** (no cases this week) or fill in the case count.
4. Add notes if needed.
5. Click **Submit**. The dialog closes; a green confirmation appears.

> **What happens if you forget?** Every Friday at 4 PM Manila time, the system checks every barangay. Missing submissions appear in the MGMT Inbox so the MHO can chase them up.

## 12 · Outbreaks

### What this is for

The system automatically detects clusters (3+ cases of the same disease in one barangay within a window) and creates an outbreak record. The MHO then advances it through the response lifecycle.

### Where to find it

Sidebar → **Registries & Surveillance** → **Outbreaks**.

### Reviewing an outbreak

1. The page lists all outbreaks with status colour: **Suspected** (amber) / **Declared** (red) / **Contained** (blue) / **Closed** (green).
2. Each row shows disease, barangay, case count, detected date.
3. (MGMT only) Click **Advance to Declared** → opens a text area asking for **Investigation findings**. Fill in and submit.
4. The status moves: **Suspected → Declared → Contained → Closed**. Each step asks for the matching note (containment actions, closure summary).

## 13 · Immunization Hub

### What this is for

Cold-chain temperature monitoring, school-based vaccinations (HPV, Td), and AEFI (Adverse Events Following Immunization) reports — all in one hub.

### Where to find it

Sidebar → **Registries & Surveillance** → **Immunization & Adverse Events**. Tabs: **Cold Chain · School · AEFI**.

### Cold Chain (daily fridge temperature log)

1. Hub → **Cold Chain** tab.
2. Click **+ Log reading**.
3. Fill in: **Device** (vaccine fridge ID), **Temperature** (°C), **Time of reading**.
4. **Save**. If the temperature is outside 2-8 °C, an alert appears in the MGMT Inbox.

### School Immunizations (HPV, Td)

1. Hub → **School** tab.
2. **+ Add encounter**.
3. Fill in: school, grade, vaccine (HPV1 / HPV2 / Td), dose number, learner name, date.
4. **Save**.

### AEFI (Adverse Event report)

1. Hub → **AEFI** tab.
2. **+ Report AEFI**.
3. Fill in: patient, vaccine, lot number, symptom, onset date, severity (**SERIOUS** or **NON_SERIOUS**).
4. **Save**.

> **Important:** SERIOUS events have a **24-hour reporting deadline** to the Center for Health Development (CHD). NON_SERIOUS events have 7 days. The system tracks both clocks.

## 15 · Mortality and death reviews

### What this is for

Recording deaths and managing the 30-day Maternal Death Review (MDR) and Perinatal Death Review (PDR) lifecycles required by DOH.

### Where to find it

Sidebar → **Registries & Surveillance** → **Mortality & Death Surveillance**. Two tabs: **Registry · Reviews**.

### Recording a death (Registry tab)

1. **Registry** tab → **+ Record Death**.
2. Fill in:
   - **Deceased name**
   - **Date of death**
   - **Age** in years (or in days for newborns/perinatal)
   - **Sex**
   - **Cause of death**
   - **Maternal death cause** (if pregnancy-related: Direct / Indirect)
   - **Fetal death (perinatal)** ✓ if before delivery
   - **Live-born early neonatal (≤6 days)** ✓
3. **Save**.

> **What happens automatically:** if the death is maternal, the system creates an MDR review with a 30-day deadline. If perinatal/neonatal, it creates a PDR review.

### Managing reviews (Reviews tab — MGMT only)

1. **Reviews** tab.
2. Each review shows status (**Pending Notify → Notified → Review Scheduled → Reviewed → Closed**) and a deadline counter.
3. Click a review row → side panel opens with current status, committee members field, findings field, recommendations field.
4. Advance the status, fill in the appropriate field, **Save**.

## 16 · Walk-in / Triage

### What this is for

Logging every patient who walks into the BHS — vital signs, danger signs, what you did, what you dispensed, where you sent them.

### Where to find it

Sidebar → **Daily Operations** → **Triage / Walk-in**.

### Logging a new walk-in

1. **+ New Triage** at top right.
2. Fill in:
   - **Patient name** (or pick from a registered patient if known)
   - **Vital signs**: BP, heart rate, respiratory rate, temperature, SpO₂
   - **Chief complaint** (cough, fever, etc.)
3. If the patient is a child, tick any **IMCI danger signs** (not able to drink, vomits everything, convulsions, lethargic). If an adult, tick adult danger signs.
4. The system **auto-suggests acuity**:
   - **EMERGENT** (red) — life-threatening, see immediately
   - **URGENT** (amber) — see within 30 minutes
   - **NON_URGENT** (gray) — routine, see in queue order
5. You can override the suggestion if your judgment differs.
6. **Disposition** dropdown — what you did:
   - **Dispensed from stock** (opens a dispense form)
   - **Referred to RHU** (creates a referral row)
   - **Admitted**
   - **Sent home**
7. **Save**.

> **What happens next:** EMERGENT and URGENT rows appear in the **Awaiting MD review** tab. Escalated walk-ins also appear in the MGMT Inbox.

## 17 · Referrals

### What this is for

The handoff between BHS and RHU (or higher facility). Every referral has a status from **PENDING → RECEIVED → COMPLETED**.

### Where to find it

Sidebar → **Services** → **Referrals**.

### If you are a Team Leader

You see "Referrals OUT" — patients you sent. Each row shows status. Click a row to view the referral letter, edit the destination, or check the outcome.

### If you are MGMT (MHO/SHA/Admin)

You see "Referrals IN" — patients sent to you.
1. Click a row → side panel opens.
2. Acknowledge by clicking **Mark RECEIVED**.
3. After the patient is treated, fill in **Outcome** and click **Mark COMPLETED**.

## 18 · Konsulta (PhilHealth)

### What this is for

PhilHealth's Konsulta primary-care benefit package. Enrolled members get free check-ups, lab tests, and meds at the BHS / RHU.

### Where to find it

Sidebar → **Services** → **Konsulta**.

### Enrolling a member

1. The page top shows a status banner: green if PhilHealth API is configured, amber if not.
2. **+ Enroll Member**.
3. Fill in:
   - **PhilHealth PIN** (12 digits)
   - **Member name**
   - **Date of birth**
   - **Contributor category** (Direct Formal / Direct Informal / Indirect Indigent / Sponsored / Lifetime)
4. **Save**. The enrollment is queued. When PhilHealth API keys are configured, queued enrollments are submitted automatically.

## 19 · Pharmacy and Inventory

### What this is for

Track every vaccine vial, every medicine bottle, who dispensed what, and what's running low.

### Where to find it

Sidebar → **Pharmacy** → tabs: **Inventory · Stockouts · Dispensings · Restock Requests**.

### Adding a new inventory item

1. **Inventory** tab → **+ Add Inventory**.
2. Fill in:
   - **Item name** (vaccine or medicine)
   - **Lot number**
   - **Expiry date**
   - **Quantity**
   - **Barangay** (where it lives)
3. **Save**.

### Looking at stockouts (MGMT only)

1. **Stockouts** tab → two columns: vaccines below threshold · medicines below threshold.
2. Filter by barangay, search by item name.
3. Click any row to see history and trigger a restock request.

### Logging a dispense

1. **Dispensings** tab → **+ Dispense**.
2. Fill in: item + lot + patient + quantity + reason.
3. **Save**. Stock decrements automatically.

## 30 · Step-by-step real situations

### Situation A — A pregnant woman walks in for her first ANC visit

1. Sidebar → **Patients → Mothers**. Search her name. If she's not listed, click **+ Add Mother**.
2. Fill in name, age, barangay, LMP date, EDC date, ANC visits = 0, TT doses = 0, phone.
3. **Save**.
4. Now click her name → her profile opens.
5. **Visit history** → **+ New Visit** → today's date, BP, weight. **Save**.
6. **Prenatal Screenings** tab in the Mothers Hub → click her row → tick what you screened today (Hep B, Anemia, etc.). **Save**.
7. Optionally, **Tools → Send SMS reminder** → confirms her next ANC visit date.

### Situation B — A child comes for the BCG vaccine

1. Sidebar → **Patients → Children**. Search the child. If new, **+ Add Child** with DOB, sex, mother, barangay, birth weight. **Save**.
2. Click the child's name → profile opens.
3. **Vaccination card** → click the empty BCG slot.
4. Fill in **Date given** and **Lot number** (read it from the vial). **Save**.
5. The slot turns green; the M1 indicator D1-02a updates automatically.

### Situation C — A patient comes in with a dog bite

1. Sidebar → **Daily Operations → Triage / Walk-in** → **+ New Triage**.
2. Enter vitals; under chief complaint pick "Animal bite". **Save**.
3. Sidebar → **Disease Surveillance → Rabies** tab → **+ New Exposure**.
4. Fill in patient details, exposure date, **Category III** (skin broken), **Treatment center: ABTC**, complete-doses unchecked for now.
5. **Save**.
6. Click the row that just appeared.
7. **A red urgent card appears at the top of the side panel** with the exact protocol: wash 15 min, vaccine D0/3/7/14/28, RIG, refer to ABTC.
8. Click **Show plain language** to read it aloud to the patient's family in simpler words.
9. Status → **REVIEWED**. Reviewer notes → "Patient given vaccine D0, RIG infiltrated, referred to Surigao Medical Center ABTC."
10. **Save**.

### Situation D — End of the month, time to submit M1

1. Sidebar → **Reports → M1 Brgy Report**.
2. Pick **Month** = the just-finished month. Year auto-fills.
3. If a draft already exists, it loads. If not, click **Create Report**.
4. Scroll through every section. Most cells are filled in for you (pre-computed from your registers).
5. Cells with a blue dot need MGMT review and edit if needed.
6. At the bottom click **Submit**. A dialog asks you to confirm — click **Yes**.
7. The report is now in the MHO's queue for approval.
8. Wait for the MHO to approve (you'll get a notification). Then **Download PDF** for your barangay's archive.

### Situation E — MHO: the Friday 4 PM PIDSR cutoff just passed

1. Sidebar → **MGMT Inbox**.
2. Filter by **PIDSR**. You'll see one row per barangay that didn't submit.
3. Click any row → it shows the barangay name, the missed week, and a link to call/SMS the TL.
4. Use the **Send SMS** button to remind the TL.
5. After they submit, the inbox row clears automatically.

### Situation F — System Administrator: someone says "the system is wrong about a patient"

1. Sidebar → **Admin → Audit Logs**.
2. Filter **entityType** = the kind of record (e.g., MOTHER, TB_PATIENT).
3. Filter **entityId** = the patient's record ID (visible in the URL of their profile page).
4. The list shows every change ever made to that record, with the user, the timestamp, the IP, and what changed (before/after JSON).
5. Click an entry to expand it. Compare beforeJson vs afterJson to see exactly what was changed.

## 31 · Frequently asked questions

**Q: I don't see a menu item that the manual mentions. Why?**
A: Your role doesn't include it. Each menu item is restricted to certain roles. If you think you should have access, ask your administrator.

**Q: The popup for an underlined acronym doesn't open when I hover.**
A: Popups open on **click**, not hover. Click the underlined term and the popup will appear below it.

**Q: I clicked "Cat III rabies" but no red recommendation card appeared.**
A: This usually means the deployed system version is older than the Recommendation Engine release. Tell your administrator: "the AI Recommendations Phase 1 build hasn't been deployed yet — the audit log dropdown won't show RECOMMENDATION_SHOWN as an option until it is."

**Q: My M1 report has lots of blank cells.**
A: M1 fills itself in from your operational records. A blank cell means no records were captured for that indicator in the period. For example, if Section C (PNC) is empty, no PNC visits were logged in the Mothers Hub PNC tab. Capture them and reload the M1 page.

**Q: A patient I registered yesterday is missing today.**
A: Most likely:
- You're looking at a different barangay (check the barangay selector at top right).
- A filter is applied (e.g., status = "Active" but the patient's status is "Lost").
- The patient was deleted — check **Audit Logs** if you suspect this.

**Q: I see "DOTS visit overdue" on a TB patient, but they came yesterday.**
A: Open their profile and update the **Next DOTS visit date**. The "overdue" pill clears when the next-visit date is set in the future.

**Q: An outbreak appeared on the Outbreaks page that I didn't create.**
A: That's normal. The system detects clusters automatically — 3+ cases of the same disease in one barangay within a window. The MHO advances it through Suspected → Declared → Contained → Closed.

**Q: I want to send the same SMS to 50 mothers.**
A: Currently SMS is sent one patient at a time from the patient profile. For bulk SMS during a campaign, use the **Campaigns** module which schedules outreach materials.

**Q: How do I get DOH guidance citations for a recommendation?**
A: Every recommendation card in the Disease Surveillance action drawer cites its DOH source at the bottom. The full glossary at **Sidebar → Glossary** also shows DOH AO numbers for every acronym.

**Q: How do I export data for a research project?**
A: Most pages have a **Download CSV** or **Download PDF** button at the top right. For deeper exports, ask the System Administrator — they can run direct database queries (with the proper data-protection sign-offs).

**Q: The system was working yesterday and now I get "Page not found" everywhere.**
A: Likely the deployment is in a bad state. Tell the administrator: "the build looks stale — please trigger a fresh redeploy." (See `docs/where-i-left-off.md` for the technical details if your administrator needs them.)

**Q: I'm new to all this. Where do I start?**
A: Start with **Today**. Look at your action queue. Click the biggest badge. Whatever it is — overdue PNC visits, vaccines due, sick children waiting — work that list first. Save records as you go. The rest of the system will fill itself in from those records.

## A note on what the system does behind your back (without your action)

Some things happen automatically every day without anyone clicking anything. You don't need to remember these, but here they are for completeness:

- **Every day at 6 AM Manila time** the system runs a sweep:
  - Checks for licenses expiring soon (workforce)
  - Checks for stockouts (inventory)
  - Checks for TB patients who missed doses
  - Checks for M1 deadlines coming up
  - Detects new disease clusters
  - Sends out due-date reminders for AEFI, FP, immunization, NCD, cervical, mhGAP follow-ups
  - **Pulls the latest news from the Caraga DOH website** and adds it to the DOH Updates feed

- **Every Friday at 4 PM Manila time** the system checks every barangay's PIDSR submission for the week. Missing barangays get listed in MGMT Inbox so the MHO can chase them.

If something happens to the schedule (server restart, etc.), the System Administrator can run the daily sweep on demand — there's a **Run Scheduler Now** button on the Settings page.
